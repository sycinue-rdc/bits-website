Preamble

    We the Computer Students of Fernandez College of Arts and Technology, recognizing the need to establish an organization that will represent it and unify students and encourage a strong union among us in order to attain unity for the advancement of our goals and inspirations, do hereby establish and proclaim these constitutions.

General Principle


Section 1. Pretense for having this Society

    The Organization is affirmed to provide each member of the Organization to be prolific of their interest by being involved in academic and extra-curricular activities for their own competitiveness, through planned group activities and projects, developing their leadership capabilities, having fruitful relationship not only with members of the organization but also with their students, faculty members and the administration and people in and out of the campus.

General Provitions


Section 1. The organization believes in the following:

  • That education is the primary need of men for him/her to attain and develop political, social, intellectual and spiritual potentials in order to be competitive in every aspect of life.
  • That education should serve as an inspiration to every mankind, students and in particular, for the improvement of this society.
  • That the computer students today shall be the leaders of the future and shall be trained in school to be responsible and serve as a good example for all.

  • To develop the organization memberâ€™s ability to be self-sufficient.
  • To enforce responsibility among the members in serving other people in general and the Organization in particular.
  • To attach the democratic principle and processes and to abide by the principle of the majority succeeds over the minority.
  • To develop academic freedom.
  • To offer help to other organizations.
